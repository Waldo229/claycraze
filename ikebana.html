Ikebana

Vessels for spatial composition.

Ikebana vessels are not containers in the conventional sense, but fields for placement. They define space, line, and interval — supporting compositions that extend beyond the object itself.

These works explore openness and asymmetry. Form is often reduced or interrupted, allowing negative space and directional movement to emerge as primary elements within the arrangement.

Ikebana Perspective

The vessel operates as a spatial framework rather than a container. Its low, open form allows stems to define vertical and directional movement, while the clay body provides a quiet ground for composition.

Irregular edges and subtle surface variation introduce tension and rhythm, supporting the dynamic balance central to ikebana practice.
