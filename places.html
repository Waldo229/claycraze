Places & People

Clay, commerce, and culture move through these places. They form part of the cultural trail behind the work.

Place Names

  • Jamestown — First permanent English settlement in North America.
  • Williamsburg — Guilds, trades, and living-history connections to pottery.
  • Charles City — Local clay, land, labor, and material history.
  • Yorktown — “Poor Potter” sites, shard stories, and kiln archaeology.
  • Smithfield — Commerce, plantations, and river transport networks.
  • The Rochambeau Route — A corridor linking Virginia to Pennsylvania and New York through the movement of people, ideas, and goods.
