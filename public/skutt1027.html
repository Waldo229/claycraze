Skutt 1027

Measured heat, pacing, and dependable transformation.

The Skutt 1027 is the electric kiln line in the studio — steady, legible, and repeatable. It provides a controlled environment for testing, refining, and carrying work through a known firing path.

Structure

An electric kiln built for consistency and reliable studio use. Its enclosed environment allows close comparison between intention, firing plan, and result.

Firing Behavior

The Skutt emphasizes pacing, rate of climb, soak, and repeatability. It is not dramatic in the way a gas kiln is, but it reveals how subtle changes in schedule affect clay and glaze over time.

Observation

This kiln is where GENE’s observational role becomes especially clear: not controlling the firing, but registering pace, tension, and fidelity to expectation as the work moves through heat.
