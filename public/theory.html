Theory

Theory is not separate from making. It is the slower, quieter companion to practice: the place where observation, history, material understanding, and reflection begin to gather into a way of seeing.

Here I keep the ideas that shape the work—thoughts on kilns, clays, glazes, tools, traditions, and the accumulated lessons that make the hand more intelligent.

If practice is the blow struck, theory is the stance, the breath, and the knowledge of where the blow belongs.

Studio

Notes on the working space itself—its arrangement, limitations, rhythms, and the practical wisdom that comes from making do.

Materials

Clay bodies, slips, glazes, and the behavior of materials under hand, tool, and fire.

Places

Landscapes, regions, and working traditions that continue to inform the eye and shape the work.

Kilns

Firing, heatwork, cooling, atmosphere, and the way time and temperature alter both clay and expectation.

GENE Project

Reflections on the reasoning machine as studio companion, registering presence, tension, and the rhythms of making.
