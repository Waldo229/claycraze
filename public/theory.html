Theory

Theory is the inward side of the work: the studio, the kilns, materials, places, and the GENE project. These are the conditions under which understanding gathers before the work appears in Practice.

This side of the site concerns process, reflection, method, and orientation. It follows the continuity of the studio story: place, matter, heat, judgment, and the larger ideas that hold them together.

Studio

The physical and material ground of the work: places, materials, tools, and techniques.

Materials

Clay bodies, slips, glazes, and the accumulated behavior of matter under heat.

Places

People, locations, and field notes that shape the larger geography of the work.

Kiln

The transforming field of heat and time: the electric Skutt and the outdoor PoorBoy gas kiln.

GENE Project

The philosophical and technical work surrounding GENE: concept, build, observation, and notes.
