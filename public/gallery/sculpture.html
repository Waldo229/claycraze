Practice

Sculpture

Sculpture allows the work to move beyond utility without losing its connection to clay as a lived material. It opens a space for gesture, presence, and a more direct engagement with form for its own sake.

I am drawn to pieces that seem discovered as much as made—forms shaped by touch, intuition, and the resistance of the material itself. In sculpture, the clay is free to become image, object, fragment, or witness.
