Practice

Ikebana

Ikebana vessels are shaped in conversation with line, space, and restraint. They are not meant to compete with the arrangement, but to support it quietly—to give the flowers a field in which gesture, pause, and asymmetry can be felt.

I am drawn to forms that hold presence without insistence: containers that remain fully themselves while allowing branch, stem, and empty space to complete the work.
