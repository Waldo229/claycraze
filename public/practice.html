Practice

Practice is where thought is tested by material. It is the daily work of shaping, trimming, firing, failing, adjusting, and beginning again until the hand learns what the mind alone cannot teach.

These pages gather the made forms: bonsai containers, ikebana vessels, sculpture, and face jugs. Together they show the work as it appears in the world, carrying both intention and accident.

If theory is reflection, practice is contact—the place where clay, fire, and judgment finally have to answer for themselves.
