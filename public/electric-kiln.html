Electric Kiln

The electric kiln is the steady heart of the studio—contained, predictable, and capable of repeatable results.

It allows for a kind of control that frees attention for other concerns: form, surface, and refinement. Heat is distributed evenly, and the firing becomes less about negotiation and more about intention.

It is a kiln of consistency, but also of quiet discipline.

View PoorBoy Kiln →

The electric kiln teaches a different kind of attention. It rewards precision, careful loading, and a clear understanding of materials.

Its reliability can be deceptive—it removes certain variables while sharpening others. Subtle differences in glaze composition, thickness, and placement become more visible, more accountable.
