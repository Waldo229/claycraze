Theory / Kiln

PoorBoy Kiln

The PoorBoy kiln is the outdoor gas line in the studio: more exposed, more atmospheric, and bound to a different rhythm of firing.

Where the electric kiln offers steadiness and repeatability, the gas kiln asks for a broader bodily attention—flame path, weather, adjustment, reduction, and the larger physical presence of the firing itself.

It is a kiln of character, but also of discipline.

See the Electric Kiln →

The PoorBoy introduces another order of judgment. It asks not only for technical control, but for responsiveness: to timing, to atmosphere, to the way heat moves through space and touches different surfaces unevenly.

That variability is part of its appeal. The gas kiln can reward patience with surfaces and transitions that feel less prescribed and more weathered into being. It is less insulated from circumstance, and therefore sometimes more revealing.
