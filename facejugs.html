Face Jugs

A Southern vernacular pottery tradition.

Face jugs occupy a unique place in the history of American ceramics. Often associated with African-American potters in the American South, these vessels combine humor, symbolism, and expressive distortion.

The ClaycrazE studio approaches the form with respect for that tradition while allowing individual character to emerge through gesture, surface, and exaggeration.
