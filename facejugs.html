Face Jugs

Southern vernacular pottery tradition.

Face jugs emerge from a long Southern vernacular tradition where function and expression meet directly in clay. These vessels often carry protective, symbolic, or personal meaning, shaped with immediacy and directness.

The work embraces irregularity and gesture — allowing features to form through touch rather than strict control. Expression is not refined away, but held and emphasized as part of the object’s presence.
